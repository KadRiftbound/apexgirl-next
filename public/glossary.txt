Top Girl Glossary
Game Structure

Home Server— Your account's primary server after the initial phase, unless there is a migration.

Abroad— Global name for major cyclical adventures like Tokyo, Bali and Roma.

Warmup— A week of preparation before an Abroad adventure, with matchmaking, scoring and important rewards.

Tokyo— The game's first Abroad adventure, on a new map with the final objective being Tokyo Tower.

Bali— The second major adventure, Abroad, follows the same general logic as Tokyo.

Roma / Ancient Rome— Third major Abroad adventure, also following the Warmup + main adventure structure.

Tokyo 2 / Bali 2 / Roma 2— Second installment of an Abroad cycle of the same name.

Tokyo 3 / Bali 3 / Roma 3— Third installment of an Abroad cycle of the same name.

Chamber— A group of 2 or 3 servers who progress together during certain Abroad adventures.

Chamber Territory— Territory built and controlled by the Chamber, useful for defense and positioning.

HQ— Group headquarters, central building for advancement, defense and several systems.

Landmark— An important building on the map, often contested, providing score, resources or area control.

Landmark First Occupation— Reward obtained upon first capturing a Landmark.

Burj Khalifa— Central objective of the origin server and major target in several server versus server wars.

Tokyo Tower— The ultimate goal of the Tokyo adventure.

Mayor— Title given to the server or group controlling a Burj Khalifa or equivalent objective.

Burj Posts— Functions distributed after the capture of the Burj Khalifa, with associated bonuses.

Server Pool— A set of servers that the game waits for before launching certain phases or cycles.

Migration— Server change after certain advanced stages of the game.

Migration Tickets— Objects that allow migration to another server.

Stats and Progression

Sing— One of the 3 main stats in the game.

Defense— One of the 3 main stats in the game, often linked to the term Defense in the game.

Economy— One of the 3 main stats in the game, very important for the economy.

Active Boost— Improvement applied directly to the artists played, such as level, skills, assets or basic stats of the Peak.

Passive Boost— An improvement that benefits all teams on the account, such as HQ, car, collection or villa.

Base Value— Base stat amount given by a system before percentage bonuses.

Percentage Increase— Multiplier applied to a raw value to boost the final stats.

Basic Stats— Base stats of an artist or system, before conditional effects.

Basic Damage Resistance— Reduction or resistance related to basic damage.

Basic Attack Damage— Damage bonus on basic attacks.

Skill Damage— Damage bonus from skills.

Skill Resistance— Resistance against damage or effects related to skills.

Damage to Player— Damage bonus dealt directly to the opposing player, very strong on objectives.

Fan Capacity— Number of fans that a team, car or bus can carry.

Rally Capacity— Maximum capacity of a rally bus.

Global Stat Boost— Stat bonus applied to the entire account or to all teams.

Main Genre— The main musical genre around which you build your team or systems.

Artists, Teams and Skills

Artist— Main unit for combat and progression in Top Girl.

UR— High artist rarity level, sometimes banned in certain events like Radio Battle.

Main Team— The most important component of your account, to prioritize for resources.

Second Team— A useful second team for defense, support, or specific content.

EDM Team— A very aggressive and dominant team in the early/mid game, strong on big objectives.

RNB Team— An accessible team, especially strong in HQ defense and very good for F2P players.

Rock Team— A versatile and solid team in the long term, with a very good kill/loss ratio.

HQ Defense— Using a team designed to defend the HQ and limit fan losses.

Solo Car— Using a team or a car for solo actions, without rallying.

Supportive Task— Support tasks, such as refilling, moving, or tactical assistance.

Driving Speed— A useful stat or skill for movement speed, especially in support roles.

3 Skills Gold— A necessary condition to unlock an artist in the Peak System.

Genre Skill— A skill that is only activated if the artist matches the genre of the song or the context.

Position Skill— A skill that is only activated if the artist is placed in the right position.

Base Skill— A skill that can always be triggered.

Boost herself— A skill whose effect depends mainly on the stats of the artist who possesses it.

Boost all artist— A skill whose effect depends on the overall stats of the entire team.

Center— A central position in a performance team, such as at the Grammy Awards.

Singer— Position as a singer in a performance team.

Dancer— Position as a dancer in a performance team.

Back— Back support position in a performance team.

Assets and Equipment

Assets— Overall name of artist equipment: jewelry, car and property.

Jewelry— The first type of asset, often the easiest to obtain.

Car— Second type of Asset, rarer and more important for stats and ability.

Property— Third type of asset, often more expensive to upgrade.

Asset Coins— Resource used to assemble the equipment.

Match Bonus— +20% bonus when an artist equips an Asset corresponding to their season or origin.

Tokyo Assets— Assets specific to Tokyo, very strong on Tokyo artists.

Exclusive Tokyo Assets— Exclusive assets obtained through Tokyo and its related events.

Auction House— Weekly auction system to obtain certain very rare items, including artists or cars.

Promote— Improvement of an asset to a higher, irreversible level.

T4 Car— Advanced tier item or resource related to the car system.

Peak, Tier and Passive Systems

Peak Level— A system that provides overall boosts and additional basic stats to certain artists.

Peak System— The entire Peak system and its progression levels.

Tier— A system that increases passive stat boosts by a percentage.

T1 / T2 / T3 / T4 / T5— Increasing Tier levels, each giving more bonuses than the previous one.

T2 (C)— First true Tier level used for multiple systems.

T3 (B)— Intermediate tier, often very interesting to unlock early.

T4 (A)— Advanced tier, very important but difficult to obtain.

T5 (A+)— Very advanced tier, rare and geared towards high-level accounts or high rankings.

Passive Car— Passive car system that boosts the stats of the entire account.

Collection— Passive system linked to musical genres, to be specialized after a common base.

Villa— Passive stats system, also linked to gender-based progression.

CEO Outfit— A recent passive system, following the same basic value + Tier logic.

HQ Skin— HQ skin providing passive bonuses that stack.

Team Feature— A late and complex system, mainly geared towards the late game and whales.

Resources and Economy

Diamonds— Premium in-game currency.

Gold— A basic resource used in many systems and upgrades.

Gold Ingots— A resource obtained primarily through the Tour, very useful at the beginning.

Interviews— Frequent source of photos of artists and their progress.

Universal Photos— Generic photos that can be used on certain artists to accelerate their rise to fame.

Relics— A system that notably influences the Grammy Award score.

Demos— A musical system whose level strongly influences certain scores, such as at the Grammy Awards.

CD— An item in the Group Shop that allows you to obtain fans.

Promotion Manuals— The Group Shop item allows you to acquire fans.

CEO Coins— Currency purchased with real money, the basis of many optimized purchases.

Monthly Card— A very profitable recurring purchase, often recommended as the best expense in the game.

Weekly Pass— Weekly pass, generally cost-effective.

Monthly Pass— Monthly pass, often good value.

Corporate Acquisition— Type of pass or offer often considered profitable.

Recharge Event— An event that provides additional bonuses during certain real-world spending.

Milestone— Reward tier reached during an event or recharge.

Shops and Boutiques

VIP Shop— VIP-level linked store, selling certain Assets among other things.

Stock Market Shop— Stock Market shop, useful for certain resources and artists.

Tokyo Shop— A shop specifically located in Tokyo, notably containing Kokoro.

Victorious Shop— Shop reserved for servers who have controlled a Burj Khalifa in City Supremacy.

Silver Shop— Intermediate store at the end of the event, depending on the server result.

Bronze Shop— End-of-event shop for servers without Burj Khalifa.

Group Shop— Group shop that resets every Monday.

Parking Wars Shop— Shop linked to Parking Wars, useful for certain resources like the HQ Tier.

Group and Coordination

Group— The main guild or alliance in the game, at the center of almost all progression.

Group Coins— Group currency used in the Group Shop.

Group Points— Points related to contributing to the group.

Personal Points— Personal points earned in certain events.

Financial Power— Power level or economic value of a group, used in certain event accesses.

Teamplay— Collective dimension of the game: coordination, timing, roles, diplomacy and macro.

Whale— A player who spends a lot of money.

F2P (Free to Play)— Player who doesn't spend, or hardly spends.

Macro game— Overall management of the server, the group, cycles and long-term objectives.

Micro game— Fine management of fights, positioning, timing and immediate decisions.

Meta— The set of choices or strategies deemed most effective at a given time.

Diplomacy— Managing relationships between groups, servers or rooms to avoid or prepare for conflicts.

Combat and War

Solo Attack— Immediate attack with a single car, quick but risky.

Rally— Group gathering before an attack, much more powerful than a solo attack.

Rally Leader— Player who opens and directs a rally.

Bus— Collective vehicle used during a rally.

Refill— The act of sending teams back into a fight or building to maintain the attack or defense.

Kills— Number of enemies defeated, often used for individual rewards.

Losses— Losses suffered in fans during the fighting.

Kill/Loss Ratio— Ratio between damage inflicted and losses suffered, very useful for judging the effectiveness of a team.

First Claim— Bonus awarded to the first group that retains control of a building for a sufficient amount of time.

Fan Losses— Number of fans lost during a fight.

Safe Zone— Initial or protected area, like the starting chamber in Tokyo.

Major Events

City Supremacy— Server versus server event in two phases: resource expenditure followed by war around the Burj Khalifa.

Ultimate CEO / Top CEO— Major weekly resource expenditure event, central to progression.

Group Battle— 6-day group event, with preparation followed by the final Rush Attack phase.

Rush Attack— Final phase of the Group Battle where you attack the opposing territories.

Radio Battle— Weekly event between 4 groups on a special map, without UR artists.

Grammy Award— Weekly music scoring event spanning 5 days and 5 genres.

Fishing Event— 7-day event where fish generate vouchers for the shop.

Fan Meeting— Mini group event every 48 hours, important for car rewards.

Tipsy Date— This event is particularly useful for exchanging certain purple equipment.

Dice Event— A recurring event, often very important for securing certain artists.

Versus Event— The confrontation event was mentioned during Tokyo.

Ultimate Group Event— Important group event during certain weeks of the adventure.

Who is the richest in Tokyo?— A sub-event in Tokyo with good rewards and scoring logic.

Parking Wars— A fashion or system linked to certain shops and resources.

Producer25— Advanced fashion or shop, particularly useful for certain villa resources.

Tokyo Events and Related Systems

Metro— Tokyo system allowing boosts and rewards to be obtained through specific progression.

Stock Market— Recurring economic system, with shop and sometimes scoring.

Stock Auction— Stock market-related sales, sometimes a source of very large rewards.

Toll 1— First opening of passage or zone after the start of Tokyo.

Hotels— An important source of gold and scoring on certain maps.

Concerts— A scoring action used in several adventures or rankings.

Stadium— Score-based or action-oriented activity on certain maps, notably Tokyo.

Fishing Event

Free Bait— Free bait that regenerates over time in the Fishing Event.

Paid Bait— Paid or premium bait is more effective for targeting the best fish.

Voucher— Fishing Event currency generated by fish.

Fish Pool— The set of fish available in a given pool.

Free Pool— Fish pool accessible with Free Bait.

Paid Pool— Fish pool accessible with Paid Bait.

Aquarium— A space where preserved fish generate vouchers.

Tank Slot— Space available in the aquarium to store a fish.

Blue Shells— Resource obtained by converting excess fish to unlock more slots.

Pity System— System guaranteeing a minimum level of rarity after a certain number of captures.

Car Part— Important reward from the Fishing Event, useful for the car.

Car Design— A significant reward from the Fishing Event, also linked to the car.

Group Battle

Battle Points— Points earned in Group Battle for the score.

Preparation Phase— Days 1 to 5 of Group Battle, focused on daily tasks.

Final Battle Phase— Day 6 of Group Battle, with decisive clashes.

Ultimate Victory— Final victory for the group with the best cumulative score.

Battle Blueprints— Resources for improving performance in Group Battle.

Master Blueprints— A rarer and more important version of the Blueprints.

Tier Rewards— Rewards unlocked in tiers in Group Battle.

VIP and Spending

VIP Level— VIP level of the account, depending mainly on the money spent.

VIP Club— System unlocked from VIP 12, with rewards and special services.

Customer Service— Game customer support, accessible in-game and via VIP Club depending on the level.

Direct Top-up— Purchasing CEO Coins directly through official channels is often more profitable than through traditional stores.
